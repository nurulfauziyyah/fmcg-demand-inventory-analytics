# FMCG Demand & Inventory Analytics

This project provides an end-to-end business analytics case study for the FMCG industry, focusing on demand patterns, promotion effectiveness, profitability, and inventory health. The analysis is designed to support data-driven decision-making for commercial, supply chain, and operations teams.

---

## üìå Project Objectives

- Understand demand trends and volatility across product categories and store locations  
- Evaluate the effectiveness of promotional activities  
- Analyze profitability trade-offs across FMCG categories  
- Assess inventory coverage and supply chain readiness  
- Translate analytical insights into actionable business recommendations  

---

## üß† Business Questions Addressed

- How does total demand evolve over time?  
- Which product categories show the highest demand volatility?  
- Which categories benefit most from promotions?  
- How do promotions affect demand across store locations?  
- How do profit margins differ across product categories?  
- How do replenishment lead times vary by location?  
- Which categories require tighter inventory control?  

---

## üìä Analysis Scope

The project is structured into five main analytical modules:

1. **Demand Structure & Variability**  
   - Yearly demand trends  
   - Weekday vs weekend effects  
   - Demand volatility by category and location  

2. **Promotion Effectiveness**  
   - Promo vs non-promo sales comparison  
   - Category-level and location-level promotion impact  

3. **Profitability & Supply Chain Analysis**  
   - Margin comparison across categories  
   - Replenishment lead time analysis by location   

4. **Inventory Health**  
   - Inventory coverage (days of inventory)  
   - Inventory differences across categories  
   - Identification of categories requiring tighter control
